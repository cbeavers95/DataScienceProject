The climate of Atlanta, GA

The city of Atlanta experiences a temperate humid subtropical climate with consistently hot muggy summers and cold winters.

Summer starts in June and ends in September, while winter runs from November to early March.
July is the hottest month. It is also the rainiest month with precipitation taking place in the form of thunderstorms. Temperatures during the day go up as high as 89.6°F (32°C) in hot summer.

Wet and chilly conditions accompany winter in Atlanta. Daytime temperatures during this time average 53.6°F (12°C) or go even down to a 42.8°F (6°C) in some occasions, while night's experience below zero conditions. January is the coldest month with occasional snowfall and ice storms.

Throughout the year, precipitation appears in the form of rain, hail, snow, or sleet. On average, April is the driest month with 3.6" (91.4mm) of rainfall.

The best time to visit Atlanta, GA

The best time to see the city is in spring or autumn. Weather conditions during this time are sweet for outdoor activities and enjoying the city shows.
During March, April, May, September, October, and November, the days are less heated with limited rainfall. Temperatures do not exceed highs of 86°F (30°C) and downs of 44.6°F (7°C).
While October stands out to be the best month to visit the city, summer is favorite with visitors.

The worst time to visit Atlanta, GA

The climatic conditions are sweltering and unwelcoming during the summer months of June, July, and August. Expect to be drenched in sweat as the city gets extremely hot.
Daytime temperatures go beyond 89.6°F (32°C) accompanied by high humidity and little rain. Expect hotel, room, and accommodation prices to swell considerably as the city draws the highest number of visitors during this time. Moving around the city becomes a tremendous challenge, too, owing to slow-moving traffic on city roads.
Atlanta also is an allergic hotbed with the experience scratchy throat and stuffy nose increasing during these blooming summer months.

Weather hazards

The weather in Atlanta is tropical, with severe lightning and thunderstorms mostly in spring and autumn. Occasional, one should be on the look out for a tropical storm that forms along the Gulf coast, bringing an abundance of rainfall, strong winds, and flash floods.

FAQ

What is the coldest month in Atlanta?

The coldest month is January, with an average high-temperature of 52.3°F (11.3°C) and an average low-temperature of 34.3°F (1.3°C).

How many days does it rain in Atlanta?

Throughout the year, in Atlanta, Georgia, there are 113.3 rainfall days, and 49.7" (1262.4mm) of precipitation is accumulated.

When does it snow in Atlanta?

January through March and December are months with snowfall in Atlanta.

How many days does it snow in Atlanta?

In Atlanta, during the entire year, snow falls for 2.1 days, and aggregates up to 2.9" (73.7mm) of snow.

When does it snow the most in Atlanta?

The month with the most snowfall is January when snow falls for 0.8 days and typically aggregates up to 1.3" (33mm) of snow.

What is the driest month in Atlanta?

The month with the least rainfall is April when the rain falls for 8.6 days and typically collects 3.4" (86.4mm) of precipitation.

What is the warmest month in Atlanta?

The warmest month is July, with an average high-temperature of 89.1°F (31.7°C) and an average low-temperature of 71.2°F (21.8°C).

What is the rainiest month in Atlanta?

The month with the most rainfall in Atlanta, Georgia, is July when the rain falls for 11.7 days and typically aggregates up to 5.3" (134.6mm) of precipitation.
